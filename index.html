FI Hacks

Life hacks to achieve financial independence and early retirement

"When can I retire?"

is an important question that people of all ages ask.

Retirement and financial independence can often seem like nothing more than a pipe dream for the average person. If you have an extremely low or a zero savings rate, you will likely have to work for the rest of your life just to survive.

Fortunately, the inverse is also true. The higher your savings rate, the faster you can retire. How much faster? All other things being equal (salary, rate of return, etc.), if you save 15% of your income you'll be able to retire in about 42 years. If you save 50% of your income, you can retire in under 17 years - a difference of 25 years! How is this possible? The math works out this way for two reasons:

  • The higher your savings rate, the more money you can invest each year for retirement. The more money you invest, the faster your nest egg will grow.
  • The higher your savings rate, the less you spend. The less you spend, the smaller your nest egg needs to be in order to retire.

Increasing your income can also increase your savings rate, but for equal amounts it is not as effective. A 5% increase in income will do less for your retirement date than a 5% decrease in spending will. Learn more.
